When to Use the Factorization Method?

Advantages of the factorization method

  • Fast and direct method when applicable
  • Doesn't require complex calculations
  • Helps understand the structure of the equation
  • Useful for solving quadratic inequalities

Limitations of the factorization method

  • Doesn't work for all quadratic equations
  • Can be difficult to apply with large or complex coefficients
  • Requires recognizing specific patterns
  • May not work for equations with irrational roots

In practice, the factorization method is particularly useful when the equation has simple integer coefficients and when we can easily find two numbers whose product is aÃ—c and sum is b.
