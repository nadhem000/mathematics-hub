When to Use the Completing the Square Method?

Advantages of the completing the square method

  • Works for all quadratic equations
  • Helps understand the derivation of the quadratic formula
  • Useful for writing quadratic equations in vertex form
  • Essential for some applications in differential calculus
  • Allows solving equations that are not easily factorable

Limitations of the completing the square method

  • Can be longer than the discriminant method
  • Requires calculations with fractions for some equations
  • Less direct than factoring for simple equations
  • Can be more prone to calculation errors

In practice, the completing the square method is particularly useful when we want to understand the structure of a quadratic equation, when working with vertex form, or when preparing the ground for the study of quadratic functions and their properties.
