Translation: Part 3

Advanced Translation Properties: Midpoint, Barycenter, Parallelism and Angles

Introduction

In this third lesson on translations, we will explore advanced properties including conservation of midpoints, barycenters, parallelism, orthogonality, and angles.

These properties demonstrate why translations are among the most fundamental transformations in geometry, preserving essential geometric relationships while moving figures in the plane.

Part VI: Conservation of the Midpoint

Translations preserve midpoints: the image of the midpoint of a segment is the midpoint of the image segment.

Activity 1: Midpoint Conservation

Figure: Triangle ABC with midpoints M, N, P
Triangle ABC with midpoints M, N, P. Translation t has vector MC.

1) Let ABC be a triangle and M, N, and P the respective midpoints of segments [BC], [AC], and [AB]. Let t be the translation of vector \(\overrightarrow{MC}\).

a) Determine t(B) and t(P).
b) Let Q be the image of A under t. Show that points M, N, and Q are collinear. Specify the position of point N on segment [MQ].

a) Since t is translation by vector \(\overrightarrow{MC}\):

\(t(B) = B'\) such that \(\overrightarrow{BB'} = \overrightarrow{MC}\)
Since M is midpoint of [BC], \(\overrightarrow{BM} = \overrightarrow{MC}\), so \(B' = M\)
\(t(P) = P'\) such that \(\overrightarrow{PP'} = \overrightarrow{MC}\)

b) Q = t(A) with \(\overrightarrow{AQ} = \overrightarrow{MC}\). Since N is midpoint of [AC], we have \(\overrightarrow{AN} = \overrightarrow{NC}\).

\(\overrightarrow{MQ} = \overrightarrow{MC} + \overrightarrow{CQ} = \overrightarrow{MC} + \overrightarrow{CA} + \overrightarrow{AQ} = \overrightarrow{MC} + \overrightarrow{CA} + \overrightarrow{MC} = 2\overrightarrow{MC} + \overrightarrow{CA}\)
\(\overrightarrow{MN} = \overrightarrow{MC} + \overrightarrow{CN} = \overrightarrow{MC} + \frac{1}{2}\overrightarrow{CA}\)

Thus \(\overrightarrow{MN} = \frac{1}{2}\overrightarrow{MQ}\), so M, N, Q are collinear with N as midpoint of [MQ].

Figure: Points I, J, O and their images I', J', O' under translation
Points I and J with midpoint O. Their images I', J', O' under translation t.

2) Let I and J be two distinct points and O the midpoint of [IJ]. Let I', J', and O' be their respective images under a translation t.

Show that O' is the midpoint of [I'J'].

Let \(\vec{u}\) be the translation vector of t. Then:

\(\overrightarrow{II'} = \vec{u}\), \(\overrightarrow{JJ'} = \vec{u}\), \(\overrightarrow{OO'} = \vec{u}\)

Since O is midpoint of [IJ], we have \(\overrightarrow{OI} + \overrightarrow{OJ} = \vec{0}\) or \(\overrightarrow{OI} = -\overrightarrow{OJ}\).

\(\overrightarrow{O'I'} = \overrightarrow{O'O} + \overrightarrow{OI} + \overrightarrow{II'} = -\vec{u} + \overrightarrow{OI} + \vec{u} = \overrightarrow{OI}\)
\(\overrightarrow{O'J'} = \overrightarrow{O'O} + \overrightarrow{OJ} + \overrightarrow{JJ'} = -\vec{u} + \overrightarrow{OJ} + \vec{u} = \overrightarrow{OJ}\)

Therefore \(\overrightarrow{O'I'} = -\overrightarrow{O'J'}\), so O' is midpoint of [I'J'].

Part VII: Conservation of Barycenter

Translations preserve barycenters: the image of a barycenter is the barycenter of the images with the same weights.

Activity 2: Barycenter Conservation

Figure: Triangle ABC with translation by vector \(\overrightarrow{BC}\)
Triangle ABC with O midpoint of [BC]. Images A', C', O' under translation t with vector \(\overrightarrow{BC}\).

1) Let ABC be a triangle, O the midpoint of [BC], and t the translation of vector \(\overrightarrow{BC}\).

Construct points A', C', and O' as images of A, C, and O respectively under t.

2) Let M be the intersection point of lines (BA') and (AO).

Show that M is the centroid (center of gravity) of triangle ABC.

Since t translates by \(\overrightarrow{BC}\), we have A' = t(A) so \(\overrightarrow{AA'} = \overrightarrow{BC}\).

In parallelogram AA'CB, (BA') and (AC) are diagonals intersecting at their midpoint.

Line (AO) is median from A to [BC]. The intersection M of (BA') and (AO) is the centroid because:

In triangle ABC, medians intersect at centroid. Since (BA') is line through B parallel to (AC) through A', and (AO) is median, their intersection divides median in ratio 2:1 from vertex.

3) Let N be the intersection point of (AC') and (A'O').

a) What does N represent for triangle A'CC'?
b) Show that N is the image of point M under translation t.

a) N is the centroid of triangle A'CC'. Since A' = t(A), C' = t(C), and t preserves barycenters.

b) Since t preserves intersections and M is centroid of ABC, N = t(M) is centroid of t(ABC) = A'BC'.

\(\overrightarrow{MN} = \overrightarrow{BC} = \vec{u}\)
Figure: Barycenter G of (I,a) and (J,b) and its image G'
Points I, J with barycenter G. Their images I', J', G' under translation t.

4) Let I and J be two points in the plane and a, b two real numbers such that a + b â‰  0. Let G be the barycenter of (I, a) and (J, b). Let I', J', and G' be the respective images of I, J, and G under a translation.

Show that G' is the barycenter of (I', a) and (J', b).

Let \(\vec{u}\) be translation vector. By definition of barycenter:

\(a\overrightarrow{GI} + b\overrightarrow{GJ} = \vec{0}\)

Under translation t: I' = I + \(\vec{u}\), J' = J + \(\vec{u}\), G' = G + \(\vec{u}\).

\(a\overrightarrow{G'I'} + b\overrightarrow{G'J'} = a(\overrightarrow{G'G} + \overrightarrow{GI} + \overrightarrow{II'}) + b(\overrightarrow{G'G} + \overrightarrow{GJ} + \overrightarrow{JJ'})\)
\(= a(-\vec{u} + \overrightarrow{GI} + \vec{u}) + b(-\vec{u} + \overrightarrow{GJ} + \vec{u}) = a\overrightarrow{GI} + b\overrightarrow{GJ} = \vec{0}\)

Thus G' is barycenter of (I', a) and (J', b).

Part VIII: Conservation of Parallelism and Orthogonality

Translations preserve parallelism and orthogonality between lines, as well as tangency between lines and circles.

Activity 3: Isometric Triangles and Quadrilaterals

Figure: Triangle MNP and its image M'N'P' under translation
Triangle MNP and its translated image M'N'P'. Show they are isometric.

1) Let MNP be a triangle and M'N'P' its image under a translation.

Show that triangles MNP and M'N'P' are isometric (congruent).

Since translation preserves distances:

\(MN = M'N'\), \(NP = N'P'\), \(PM = P'M'\)

Therefore by SSS (Side-Side-Side) criterion, triangles MNP and M'N'P' are congruent.

Figure: Rectangle ABCD and its image A'B'C'D' under translation
Rectangle ABCD and its translated image A'B'C'D'.

2) Let ABCD be a quadrilateral and A'B'C'D' its image under a translation.

Determine the nature of quadrilateral A'B'C'D' in each case:
a) ABCD is a rectangle.
b) ABCD is a rhombus.

a) If ABCD is a rectangle, then A'B'C'D' is also a rectangle because:

  • Translation preserves lengths, so opposite sides remain equal
  • Translation preserves angles, so all angles remain 90Â°

b) If ABCD is a rhombus, then A'B'C'D' is also a rhombus because:

  • Translation preserves lengths, so all sides remain equal
  • Translation preserves parallelism, so opposite sides remain parallel

3) What can we say about the image of a polygon under a translation?

The image of any polygon under a translation is:

  • Congruent to the original polygon (same shape and size)
  • All corresponding sides are parallel
  • All corresponding angles are equal
  • The orientation is preserved

Part IX: Conservation of Angles

Translations preserve angle measures: the image of an angle is an angle with the same measure.

Activity 4: Angle Conservation

Figure: Angle xOy and its image x'O'y' under translation
Angle âˆ xOy and its translated image âˆ x'O'y'. Show they are equal.

Let t be a translation of vector \(\vec{u}\) and [Ox, Oy] an angle sector. Let [O'x') and [O'y') be the respective images of half-lines [Ox) and [Oy) under translation t.

Show that \(\angle xOy = \angle x'O'y'\).

Let \(\vec{u} = \overrightarrow{OO'}\). Then for any point X on [Ox) and Y on [Oy), we have:

\(X' = t(X) = X + \vec{u}\), \(Y' = t(Y) = Y + \vec{u}\)

Consider triangles OXY and O'X'Y'. We have:

\(\overrightarrow{O'X'} = \overrightarrow{O'O} + \overrightarrow{OX} + \overrightarrow{XX'} = -\vec{u} + \overrightarrow{OX} + \vec{u} = \overrightarrow{OX}\)
\(\overrightarrow{O'Y'} = \overrightarrow{OY}\) (similarly)

Thus triangles OXY and O'X'Y' have corresponding sides equal, so they are congruent. Therefore, \(\angle XOY = \angle X'O'Y'\).

Comprehensive Summary

Conservation of Barycenter

  • Translation preserves the midpoint.
  • Translation preserves the barycenter.

Conservation of Parallelism, Orthogonality and Contact

  • The images under a translation of two parallel lines are two parallel lines.
  • The images under a translation of two perpendicular lines are two perpendicular lines.
  • If a line and a circle are tangent, then their images under a translation are tangent.

Conservation of Angles

Translations preserve angle measures: the image of an angle is an angle with the same measure.
