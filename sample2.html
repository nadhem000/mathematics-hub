Pythagorean Theorem

Introduction

The Pythagorean Theorem is one of the most fundamental concepts in geometry, describing the relationship between the sides of a right triangle.

Pythagorean Theorem

In a right triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides.

a^2 + b^2 = c^2

where \(c\) is the length of the hypotenuse, and \(a\) and \(b\) are the lengths of the other two sides.

Example Problem

Example 1: Finding the Hypotenuse

A right triangle has legs of length \(a = 3\) and \(b = 4\). Find the length of the hypotenuse.

Step 1: Apply Pythagorean theorem

\(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)

Step 2: Substitute known values

\(3^2 + 4^2 = c^2\)

Step 3: Calculate squares

\(9 + 16 = c^2\)

Step 4: Simplify

\(25 = c^2\)

Step 5: Take square root

\(c = \sqrt{25} = 5\)

The hypotenuse has length \(5\) units.

Example 2: Finding a Leg

A right triangle has a hypotenuse of length \(c = 13\) and one leg of length \(a = 5\). Find the length of the other leg.

Step 1: Apply Pythagorean theorem

\(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)

Step 2: Substitute known values

\(5^2 + b^2 = 13^2\)

Step 3: Calculate squares

\(25 + b^2 = 169\)

Step 4: Isolate \(b^2\)

\(b^2 = 169 - 25 = 144\)

Step 5: Take square root

\(b = \sqrt{144} = 12\)

The other leg has length \(12\) units.

Vector Geometry Exercise

Given two vectors in 2D space:

\(\vec{v} = 3\hat{i} + 4\hat{j} \quad \text{and} \quad \vec{w} = 6\hat{i} - 2\hat{j}\)

Find the magnitude of the resultant vector \(\vec{r} = \vec{v} + \vec{w}\) using:

\(|\vec{r}| = \sqrt{(v_x + w_x)^2 + (v_y + w_y)^2}\)

Also calculate the unit vector \(\hat{u}\) in the direction of \(\vec{r}\):

\(\hat{u} = \frac{\vec{r}}{|\vec{r}|}\)

Step 1: Find the resultant vector \(\vec{r}\)

\(\vec{r} = \vec{v} + \vec{w} = (3 + 6)\hat{i} + (4 - 2)\hat{j} = 9\hat{i} + 2\hat{j}\)

Step 2: Calculate the magnitude \(|\vec{r}|\)

\(|\vec{r}| = \sqrt{9^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{81 + 4} = \sqrt{85} \approx 9.22\)

Step 3: Find the unit vector \(\hat{u}\)

\(\hat{u} = \frac{9\hat{i} + 2\hat{j}}{\sqrt{85}} \approx \frac{9}{\sqrt{85}}\hat{i} + \frac{2}{\sqrt{85}}\hat{j}\)

Complex Mathematical Notation

Solve the following equation using various mathematical symbols:

\(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\int_{0}^{x} e^{-t^2} dt\right] = e^{-x^2}\)

Prove that:

\(\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists \delta > 0 \ni |x - a| < \delta \Rightarrow |f(x) - f(a)| < \epsilon\)

Evaluate the infinite series:

\(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n(n+1)} = 1\)

Step 1: Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

\(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\int_{0}^{x} e^{-t^2} dt\right] = e^{-x^2}\)

This is a direct application of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.

Step 2: Epsilon-Delta Definition of Continuity

\(\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists \delta > 0 \ni |x - a| < \delta \Rightarrow |f(x) - f(a)| < \epsilon\)

This is the formal definition of continuity at point \(a\).

Step 3: Telescoping Series

\(\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n(n+1)} = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{n} - \frac{1}{n+1}\right)\)
\(= \left(1 - \frac{1}{2}\right) + \left(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3}\right) + \left(\frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{4}\right) + \cdots = 1\)
