Application 1: Proportionality Problems

A phone plan costs $20 per month plus $0.10 per minute. For how many minutes will the cost be less than $50?

Let m = number of minutes

Inequation: 20 + 0.10m < 50

Subtract 20: 0.10m < 30

Divide by 0.10: m < 300

The cost will be less than $50 for fewer than 300 minutes.

Application 2: Geometry Problems

The length of a rectangle is 5 cm more than its width. If the perimeter is less than 30 cm, what are the possible dimensions?

Let w = width, then length = w + 5

Perimeter: 2(w + w + 5) < 30

Simplify: 2(2w + 5) < 30

Distribute: 4w + 10 < 30

Subtract 10: 4w < 20

Divide by 4: w < 5

The width must be less than 5 cm, and the length less than 10 cm.

Application 3: Price Comparison

Company A charges $50 plus $0.20 per unit. Company B charges $30 plus $0.30 per unit. For how many units is Company A cheaper?

Let x = number of units

Inequation: 50 + 0.20x < 30 + 0.30x

Subtract 30: 20 + 0.20x < 0.30x

Subtract 0.20x: 20 < 0.10x

Divide by 0.10: 200 < x

Company A is cheaper for more than 200 units.
