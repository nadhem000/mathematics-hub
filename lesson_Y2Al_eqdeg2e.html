Summary and Key Points

Key Points to Remember

  • Always start by writing the inequality in standard form
  • Factor the quadratic expression when possible
  • Use sign tables for systematic analysis
  • Pay attention to strict vs. non-strict inequalities
  • Check special cases (perfect squares, no real roots)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting to reverse inequality when multiplying by negative
  • Incorrectly identifying critical points
  • Mishandling boundary points
  • Not checking special cases

With practice, solving quadratic inequalities algebraically becomes a systematic process that you can apply confidently to any problem.
