Translation: Part 2

Translation Properties and Applications

Introduction

In this lesson, we will study the fundamental properties of translations, including distance preservation, conservation of alignment, and how translations affect lines, segments, and half-lines.

These properties are essential for understanding geometric transformations and their applications in problem-solving.

Part III: Characteristic Property of Translations

A translation is uniquely characterized by its property of preserving distances between points.

Activity 1: Distance Preservation

Figure: Translation preserving distance MN = M'N'
Points M and N are translated by vector u to M' and N'. Click buttons to show constructions.

1) Let M and N be two points in the plane with respective images M' and N' under a translation of vector \(\vec{u}\). Show that \(M'N' = MN\).

By definition of translation:

\(\overrightarrow{MM'} = \vec{u}\) and \(\overrightarrow{NN'} = \vec{u}\)

Therefore:

\(\overrightarrow{M'N'} = \overrightarrow{M'M} + \overrightarrow{MN} + \overrightarrow{NN'}\)
\(\overrightarrow{M'N'} = -\vec{u} + \overrightarrow{MN} + \vec{u} = \overrightarrow{MN}\)

Thus \(\overrightarrow{M'N'} = \overrightarrow{MN}\), so \(M'N' = MN\).

2) Let f be an application of the plane, M and N any two points, and M' and N' their respective images under f. Show that if \(M'N' = MN\) for all M, N, then f is a translation.

Let A be a fixed point and A' = f(A). For any point M with image M' = f(M), we have:

\(A'M' = AM\) (by hypothesis with N = A)

Consider triangle AMM'. Since AM = A'M' and the transformation preserves distances, the only possibility is that quadrilateral AA'M'M is a parallelogram. Therefore:

\(\overrightarrow{MM'} = \overrightarrow{AA'}\) for all M

This means f is the translation of vector \(\overrightarrow{AA'}\).

Consequences:

  • For any points M and N with respective images M' and N' under a translation, we have \(MN = M'N'\). We say a translation preserves distances.
  • For any distinct points M and N with respective images M' and N' under a translation, the lines (MN) and (M'N') are parallel.

Activity 2: Identifying a Translation

Figure: Application f defined by vector sum
Points A, B, C are fixed. M is mapped to M' such that \(\overrightarrow{MM'} = \overrightarrow{MA} + \overrightarrow{MB} - 2\overrightarrow{MC}\).

Let A, B, and C be three points in the plane and let f be the application defined by:

f: P ‚Üí P
M ‚Ü¶ M' such that \(\overrightarrow{MM'} = \overrightarrow{MA} + \overrightarrow{MB} - 2\overrightarrow{MC}\)

Show that f is a translation and determine its vector.

Let's express \(\overrightarrow{MM'}\) in terms of fixed vectors:

\(\overrightarrow{MM'} = \overrightarrow{MA} + \overrightarrow{MB} - 2\overrightarrow{MC}\)

Using Chasles relation:

\(\overrightarrow{MA} = \overrightarrow{MG} + \overrightarrow{GA}\) where G is the midpoint of [AB]
\(\overrightarrow{MB} = \overrightarrow{MG} + \overrightarrow{GB}\)

Since G is the midpoint, \(\overrightarrow{GA} + \overrightarrow{GB} = \vec{0}\). Also, \(\overrightarrow{GB} = -\overrightarrow{GA}\).

\(\overrightarrow{MA} + \overrightarrow{MB} = 2\overrightarrow{MG}\)

Thus:

\(\overrightarrow{MM'} = 2\overrightarrow{MG} - 2\overrightarrow{MC} = 2(\overrightarrow{MG} - \overrightarrow{MC}) = 2\overrightarrow{CG}\)

Since \(\overrightarrow{CG}\) is a fixed vector (independent of M), f is the translation of vector \(2\overrightarrow{CG}\).

Part IV: Conservation of Alignment

Translations preserve collinearity: if three points are aligned, their images under a translation are also aligned.

Activity 3: Collinearity Preservation

Figure: Collinear points M, N, P and their images
Points M, N, P are collinear. Their images M', N', P' under translation t are also collinear.

1) Let M, N, and P be three collinear points, and let M', N', and P' be their respective images under a translation t of vector \(\vec{u}\). Show that M', N', and P' are collinear.

Since M, N, P are collinear, there exists a real number k such that:

\(\overrightarrow{NP} = k \cdot \overrightarrow{NM}\)

Under translation t:

\(\overrightarrow{N'P'} = \overrightarrow{NP}\) (since translation preserves vectors)
\(\overrightarrow{N'M'} = \overrightarrow{NM}\)

Therefore:

\(\overrightarrow{N'P'} = k \cdot \overrightarrow{N'M'}\)

This shows that N', P', and M' are collinear.

2) Let A, B, and C be three points on a line Œî. Let I and J be two points not on Œî.

Figure: Constructing parallelograms AIJH, BIJK, CIJL
Construct points H, K, L such that AIJH, BIJK, and CIJL are parallelograms.

a) Construct points H, K, and L such that AIJH, BIJK, and CIJL are parallelograms.
b) Show that points H, K, and L are collinear.

a) Construction:

  • H is such that \(\overrightarrow{AH} = \overrightarrow{IJ}\) (so AIJH is a parallelogram)
  • K is such that \(\overrightarrow{BK} = \overrightarrow{IJ}\) (so BIJK is a parallelogram)
  • L is such that \(\overrightarrow{CL} = \overrightarrow{IJ}\) (so CIJL is a parallelogram)

b) All three points H, K, L are obtained by translating A, B, C by the same vector \(\overrightarrow{IJ}\). Since A, B, C are collinear on Œî, their images H, K, L under the same translation are also collinear.

\(\overrightarrow{HK} = \overrightarrow{AB}\) and \(\overrightarrow{KL} = \overrightarrow{BC}\)

Since A, B, C are aligned, vectors \(\overrightarrow{AB}\) and \(\overrightarrow{BC}\) are colinear, hence H, K, L are aligned.

Part V: Image of a Line, Segment, and Half-Line

Translations transform lines into parallel lines, segments into isometric segments, and half-lines into half-lines.

Activity 4: Images of Geometric Objects

Figure: Parallelogram ABCD and translation by vector \(\overrightarrow{AC}\)
Parallelogram ABCD with Œî parallel to (AC) through B. Determine images of lines under translation t with vector \(\overrightarrow{AC}\).

Let ABCD be a parallelogram and Œî the line parallel to (AC) passing through B. Consider the translation t with vector \(\overrightarrow{AC}\).

1) Determine the image of each of the lines (AB), (AD), and Œî under translation t.
2) Construct the image of line (DB) and the image of line (BC) under translation t.

1) Image of lines:

  • Image of (AB): Since A maps to C and B maps to D (in parallelogram ABCD, \(\overrightarrow{AB} = \overrightarrow{DC}\)), the image of line (AB) is line (CD).
  • Image of (AD): Since A maps to C and D maps to B (since \(\overrightarrow{AD} = \overrightarrow{CB}\)), the image of line (AD) is line (CB).
  • Image of Œî: Since Œî is parallel to (AC) and passes through B, and translation t sends B to a point B' such that \(\overrightarrow{BB'} = \overrightarrow{AC}\). But in parallelogram ABCD, \(\overrightarrow{AC} = \overrightarrow{BD}\), so B' = D. Thus the image of Œî is the line through D parallel to (AC).

2) Construction:

  • Image of (DB): Since D maps to B and B maps to D, line (DB) maps to itself (but with reversed orientation).
  • Image of (BC): Since B maps to D and C maps to A (since \(\overrightarrow{BC} = \overrightarrow{AD}\)), the image of line (BC) is line (DA).

Comprehensive Summary

Characteristic Property

Let t be an application of the plane and M, N any two points with respective images M', N' under t.

(t is a translation) ‚áî (M'N' = MN for all M, N)

This is the defining characteristic of translations: they preserve distances.

Conservation of Alignment

The images of collinear points under a translation are also collinear. Translations preserve collinearity.

Images of Geometric Objects

  • The image of a line under a translation is a line parallel to the original line.
  • The image of a segment under a translation is a segment that is isometric (equal in length) to the original segment.
  • The image of a half-line under a translation is a half-line.
