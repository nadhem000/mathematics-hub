Homothety: Part 6

Advanced Exercises and Applications

Introduction

In this final lesson on homothety, we explore advanced applications combining homothety with other geometric transformations (translation, barycenter, symmetry). The exercises demonstrate how homothety can be used to solve complex geometric problems involving triangles, circles, and trapezoids.

These exercises integrate multiple concepts and require a deep understanding of homothety properties, barycentric coordinates, and geometric constructions.

Exercise 1

Let ABC be a triangle. E is the image of A by the translation of vector \(\frac{1}{4}\overrightarrow{BC}\). F is the barycenter of weighted points (A, 4) and (C, 1).

Figure: Triangle ABC with points E and F
Triangle ABC. E = translation of A by (1/4)BC. F = barycenter of (A,4) and (C,1).

a) Draw a figure.

b) Show that F is the image of E by a homothety with center B. Determine the ratio.

Step 1: Express E and F using vectors

  • E: \(\overrightarrow{AE} = \frac{1}{4}\overrightarrow{BC}\)
  • F: barycenter of (A,4) and (C,1): \(\overrightarrow{AF} = \frac{1}{5}\overrightarrow{AC}\)

Step 2: Express \(\overrightarrow{BE}\) and \(\overrightarrow{BF}\)

  • \(\overrightarrow{BE} = \overrightarrow{BA} + \overrightarrow{AE} = \overrightarrow{BA} + \frac{1}{4}\overrightarrow{BC}\)
  • \(\overrightarrow{BF} = \overrightarrow{BA} + \overrightarrow{AF} = \overrightarrow{BA} + \frac{1}{5}\overrightarrow{AC}\)

Step 3: Show \(\overrightarrow{BF} = k\overrightarrow{BE}\)

  • We have: \(\overrightarrow{AC} = \overrightarrow{AB} + \overrightarrow{BC} = -\overrightarrow{BA} + \overrightarrow{BC}\)
  • Thus: \(\overrightarrow{BF} = \overrightarrow{BA} + \frac{1}{5}(-\overrightarrow{BA} + \overrightarrow{BC}) = \frac{4}{5}\overrightarrow{BA} + \frac{1}{5}\overrightarrow{BC}\)
  • And: \(\overrightarrow{BE} = \overrightarrow{BA} + \frac{1}{4}\overrightarrow{BC}\)
  • We want \(\overrightarrow{BF} = k\overrightarrow{BE}\)
  • This gives: \(\frac{4}{5}\overrightarrow{BA} + \frac{1}{5}\overrightarrow{BC} = k(\overrightarrow{BA} + \frac{1}{4}\overrightarrow{BC})\)
  • Equating coefficients: \(\frac{4}{5} = k\) and \(\frac{1}{5} = \frac{k}{4}\)
  • Both give \(k = \frac{4}{5}\)
\(\overrightarrow{BF} = \frac{4}{5}\overrightarrow{BE}\) â‡’ F is image of E by homothety h(B, 4/5)
