Basic Concept

Quadratic Function Behavior

The graph of a quadratic function y = ax¬≤ + bx + c is a parabola. The sign of the coefficient a determines the direction of the parabola:

  • If a > 0, the parabola opens upward
  • If a < 0, the parabola opens downward

The roots of the equation ax¬≤ + bx + c = 0 divide the x-axis into intervals where the quadratic expression maintains a constant sign.

Advantages and Limitations

Advantages

  • Works for all quadratic inequalities
  • Provides exact solution sets
  • Systematic approach that always works
  • Helps understand the relationship between equations and inequalities

Limitations

  • Can be time-consuming for complex coefficients
  • Requires careful handling of inequality signs
  • May be less intuitive than graphical methods for some students
  • Requires solving the corresponding quadratic equation first

Important Note

The algebraic method for solving quadratic inequalities is the most reliable technique. While graphical methods can provide visual intuition, the algebraic method gives precise solution sets and works for all cases, including those with no real roots.
