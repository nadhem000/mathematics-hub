1st Degree Equations II: Transposition Method

Introduction to Transposition Method

The transposition method is an alternative technique for solving first-degree equations. It involves directly moving terms from one side to the other of the equation by changing their sign.

What is Transposition?

Transposition is a rule that allows moving a term from one side to the other of the equal sign by changing its operation:

  • A positive term becomes negative when transposed
  • A negative term becomes positive when transposed
  • A multiplicative factor becomes a divisor when transposed
  • A divisor becomes a multiplicative factor when transposed

This method is particularly effective for simple equations and often allows arriving at the solution more quickly.

Both methods are mathematically equivalent. The transposition method is simply a mental shortcut of the traditional method. The choice between the two depends on your personal preferences.

Transposition Rules

Rule 1: Transposition of Additive/Subtractive Terms

When transposing a term from one side to the other of the equation, we change its sign:

If x + a = b, then x = b - a

If x - a = b, then x = b + a

When to Use the Transposition Method?

Advantages of the transposition method

  • Faster for simple equations
  • Allows direct visualization of the solving process
  • Reduces the number of intermediate steps

Limitations of the transposition method

  • Can be less intuitive for beginners
  • Requires good understanding of inverse operations
  • Can be more difficult to apply for complex equations

In practice, many students and professionals use a combination of both methods, choosing the one that seems most appropriate for each equation.
