Fundamental Rules for Comparing Real Numbers

Sign Rules for Products and Quotients

The product of two real numbers is positive if both numbers have the same sign.

The product of two real numbers is negative if the two numbers have opposite signs.

The quotient of two nonzero real numbers is positive if both numbers have the same sign.

The quotient of two nonzero real numbers is negative if the two numbers have opposite signs.

Summary

Key Concepts

  • Real numbers follow specific rules for comparison based on their signs and magnitudes
  • The product or quotient of two numbers is positive if they have the same sign, negative if they have opposite signs
  • Inequalities follow transitivity and can be manipulated by adding or multiplying (with attention to sign changes)
  • Absolute value represents distance from zero on the number line and follows specific algebraic rules
  • Intervals provide a way to represent sets of real numbers with specific boundaries

Mastering the comparison of real numbers is fundamental to advancing in algebra and will be particularly important when working with functions, inequalities, and calculus concepts.
